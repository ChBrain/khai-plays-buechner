# The Society for Human Rights: Reference

## Line of Work

Georg Büchner's founding of the secret _Gesellschaft der Menschenrechte_ in Gießen and Darmstadt (1834), staged as a **conspirator's attempt to organize a revolutionary cell**. The play models the political mobilization of students and citizens under the banner of human rights, bridging the gap between theoretical radicalism and the printing of _The Hessian Courier_. The system focuses on the tension between konspirative trust, the written oath, and the betrayal that breaks the alliance.

## Origin

The historical founding of the _Gesellschaft der Menschenrechte_ by Georg Büchner in Gießen in March 1834, its expansion to Darmstadt, the alliance with Friedrich Ludwig Weidig, and the eventual dismantling of the society by the Hessian police.

| Source            | Key Work / Event                       | Scope                                                                                               |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The Frankfurt Wachensturm**: The production refuses to stage the historical aftermath or the previous Frankfurt revolt, delegating the broader German revolutionary context to the verbal debates.
- **The print production details**: The production refuses to model the actual printing press mechanics of the Courier, delegating them to the finished manuscript leaving the secret hideout.
- **The trials and prison terms**: The production refuses to show the trials of Becker and Minnigerode, terminating at the moment the Gießen cell is compromised.
