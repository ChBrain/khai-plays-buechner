# Weidig's Martyrdom: Reference

## Line of Work

Friedrich Ludwig Weidig's imprisonment, interrogation, and death in the Darmstadt house of correction (1834-1837), staged as a **conspirator's terminal silence under state terror**. The play models the psychological and physical isolation of Weidig as he resists the inquisitorial pressure of Konrad Georgi. The system focuses on the tension between the legal authority of the inquisitor and the silent moral resistance of the dying pastor.

## Origin

The historical trial records, interrogation protocols, and reports concerning the investigation against Friedrich Ludwig Weidig and other members of the _Gesellschaft der Menschenrechte_ in Hesse (1834-1837).

| Source                      | Key Work / Event                               | Scope                                                                                     |
| :-------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Friedrich Ludwig Weidig** | _The Hessian Courier_ co-authorship (1834)     | The theological and political agitations that provoked the state's retaliation.           |
| **Konrad Georgi**           | Interrogation protocols (Darmstadt, 1835-1837) | The inquisitorial records documenting the psychological coercion and torture.             |
| **August Becker**           | Trial testimonies and memoirs                  | The testimonies of the fellow conspirator documenting the conditions of Darmstadt prison. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The wider trial of the conspirators**: The production refuses to stage the legal trials of the other Hessian conspirators, delegating the broader political repression to the isolation of Weidig's cell.
- **The family's grief**: The production refuses to show the visits or petitions of Weidig's wife and children, delegating his personal relations to the solitary reading of the Bible.
- **The forensic autopsy**: The production refuses to resolve the historical controversy of whether Weidig committed suicide or was murdered, delegating his death directly to the bloodstain on the floor and the presence of the shaving knife.
