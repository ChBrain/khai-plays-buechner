# The Wanted Poster: Reference

## Line of Work

Georg Büchner's political persecution in Hesse (March–June 1835), staged as a **race against the state's slow bureaucratization of capture**. The play models the three critical weeks of confinement in Darmstadt where Büchner wrote _Danton's Death_ (_Dantons Tod_) in his parental home to fund his escape, contrasting Karl Minnigerode's immediate physical captivity with the state's late issuing of the wanted poster (_Steckbrief_). The system focuses on the tension between the physical border, the written word under police watch, and the final late warrant.

## Origin

The historical arrest of Karl Minnigerode at the Gießen gate with copies of _The Hessian Courier_ (August 1834), Büchner's subsequent isolation and writing of _Dantons Tod_ in Darmstadt (February 1835), his flight to Strasbourg, and the official wanted poster issued by the Grand Ducal Office on June 13, 1835.

| Source            | Key Work / Event                | Scope                                                                                                        |
| :---------------- | :------------------------------ | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hessian State** | _Der Steckbrief_ (June 1835)    | The official arrest warrant published in the Frankfurter Oberpostamtszeitung against Georg Büchner.          |
| **Georg Büchner** | Writing of _Dantons Tod_ (1835) | The composition of his debut play in the Darmstadt laboratory, written to secure funds for political escape. |
| **History**       | Arrest of Minnigerode (1834)    | The capture at the Gießen gate which began the dismantling of the Gießen Society for Human Rights.           |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The trial of Weidig and Minnigerode**: The production refuses to stage the long, brutal torture and interrogation chambers of Friedberg and Gießen, delegating it to the symbolic presence of Martin Georgi's ledger and Minnigerode's silent imprisonment.
- **The publishing of Danton's Death**: The production refuses to model Karl Gutzkow's editing and Sauerländer's printing of the drama in Frankfurt, delegating it to the finished manuscript leaving the house.
- **Strasbourg life**: The production refuses to show Büchner's life after crossing the border, terminating exactly when the wanted poster is posted on the Darmstadt walls.
