# The Young Germany Censorship: Reference

## Line of Work

Karl Gutzkow's publishing efforts, the censorship of _Danton's Death_, and the proscription of the Young Germany literary movement by the German Federal Assembly in December 1835, staged as a **clash of state-mandated silence and editorial compromise**. The play models the commercial and political pressures that forced Gutzkow to redact Büchner's text. The system focuses on the tension between the absolute decree of the chancellor and the survival of the censored manuscript.

## Origin

The historical publishing records, censored editions of _Dantons Tod_ in the journal _Phönix. Frühlings-Zeitung für Deutschland_ (1835), and the federal decrees issued by the German Confederation against Young Germany.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                                         | Scope                                                                    |
| :----------------- | :------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Georg Büchner**  | _Danton's Death_ manuscript (1835)                       | The raw, uncensored play submitted to Gutzkow to finance his flight.     |
| **Karl Gutzkow**   | Phoenix serialization and correspondence (1835)          | The editorial revisions and public defense of the new literature.        |
| **Deutscher Bund** | Federal Decree banning Young Germany (December 10, 1835) | The imperial proscription outlawing all works published by the movement. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The historical Frankfurt police raid**: The production refuses to show the physical arrest of Karl Gutzkow by the city gendarmes, delegating the state's force to the signing of the federal decree and the silence of the office.
- **The Parisian reactions**: The production refuses to show the reception of the Young Germany refugees in France, delegating the international escape to the Straßburger Asyl.
- **The full stage production of Danton**: The production refuses to perform the play _Danton's Death_ itself, delegating its theatrical form to the paper manuscript and the printed columns of the Phoenix journal.
