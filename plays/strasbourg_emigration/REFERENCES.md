# The Strasbourg Emigration: Reference

## Line of Work

Georg Büchner's exile in Strasbourg, his relationship with Minna Jaeglé, and the intellectual community of German refugees (1835-1836), staged as a **tension between domestic sanctuary and political exile**. The play models the quiet safety of the Jaeglé parsonage contrasted with the heated disputes of exiles in the local taverns. The system focuses on the emotional and physical recovery of the writer, his engagement, and the constant threat of bad news from the home country.

## Origin

The historical correspondence between Georg Büchner and Minna Jaeglé, the letters to his family, and the testimonies of fellow refugees like Wilhelm Schulz in Strasbourg (1835-1836).

| Source                   | Key Work / Event                                  | Scope                                                                                       |
| :----------------------- | :------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Georg Büchner**        | _Letters to Minna Jaeglé_ (Strasbourg, 1835-1836) | The emotional record of his relief, his affection for Minna, and his homesickness.          |
| **Wilhelm Schulz**       | Memoirs of the Emigration                         | The reports of the debates and living conditions of the German democratic circle in France. |
| **Hessian State Trials** | Reports of arrests and trials in Darmstadt (1835) | The political background that cast a shadow over the refugee community.                     |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The French municipal politics**: The production refuses to show the Strasbourg police or administration's surveillance of the refugees, delegating international relations to the safety of the parsonage and the streets.
- **The details of the Frankfurt Wachensturm**: The production refuses to stage the historical events of the 1833 revolt, delegating the memory of the failed uprising to the discussions in the tavern.
- **Büchner's scientific work**: The production refuses to show the dissecting room or academic writing of the barbel memoir, delegating his intellectual work to the political debates with Schulz.
