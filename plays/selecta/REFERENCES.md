# The Selecta: Reference

## Line of Work

Georg Büchner's gymnasium essays and speeches (Darmstadt, 1829–30), staged as an **adolescent's rehearsal of voluntary death within academic boundaries**. The play models the rhetorical preparation of a schoolboy in the elite "Selecta" class, who addresses three aspects of self-destruction and sacrifice before his teachers. The system focuses on the transition from classical, sanctioned oratory to the dangerous, real-world application of fatalism.

## Origin

The three surviving school texts written by Georg Büchner at the Ludwig-Georgs-Gymnasium in Darmstadt:

1. _Helden-Tod der vierhundert Pforzheimer_ (delivered June 1830) — a speech on collective sacrificial death in war.
2. _Verteidigung des Cato von Utika_ (delivered 1830) — a defense of Cato's philosophical suicide.
3. _Rezension eines Aufsatzes_ (written 1829) — a review of a classmate's essay examining suicide from the perspective of Christian ethics.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Büchner's childhood**: The production refuses to show Büchner's early family life or childhood education, focusing strictly on his final years at the Darmstadt gymnasium.
- **The classmate's actual suicide**: The production refuses to stage the physical death or suicide of the classmate, delegating it to the manuscript of his essay and the symbolic presence of the graveyard.
- **The subsequent university studies**: The production refuses to show his medical studies in Strasbourg or Gießen, ending at the moment the Reifezeugnis (graduation certificate) is signed.
