# The Philosophy Scripts: Reference

## Line of Work

Georg Büchner's lecture preparation on the history of modern philosophy (Strasbourg/Zurich, 1836), staged as a **fugitive's attempt to establish a materialist philosophy within academic frameworks**. The play models the quiet, intellectual transition from scientific dissection to philosophical monism, translating Spinoza and Descartes into a German course. The system focuses on the tension between the physical mortality of the lecturer and the eternal nature of the philosophical substance he writes about.

## Origin

The surviving lecture drafts and notes prepared by Georg Büchner during his exile in Strasbourg and his early months in Zurich (1836), focusing on René Descartes' rationalism and Baruch Spinoza's monism.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                                 | Scope                                                                                               |
| :----------------- | :----------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Georg Büchner**  | Vorlesungsskripte zur neueren Philosophie (1836) | The lecture notes analyzing Descartes' dualism and Spinoza's substance.                             |
| **René Descartes** | _Meditationes de Prima Philosophia_ (1641)       | The rationalist foundation of modern philosophy, defining the separation of mind and body.          |
| **Baruch Spinoza** | _Ethica, ordine geometrico demonstrata_ (1677)   | The monist philosophy defining God and Nature as one single, infinite substance (Deus sive Natura). |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The Zurich faculty reception**: The production refuses to show the university senate's review of Büchner's trial lecture or his formal appointment, delegating academic success to the empty lecture hall and the course announcement.
- **The Cartesian-Spinozist debates**: The production refuses to stage historical debates between Cartesian and Spinozist schools, delegating their philosophical tension directly to the two drafted manuscripts.
- **The physical decay of typhus**: The production refuses to show the clinical details of Büchner's final illness, ending at the moment the lectures are silenced and the manuscripts remain unread.
