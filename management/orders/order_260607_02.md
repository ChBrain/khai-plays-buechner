# Management Order: Staging of Lenz

## Direction

Georg Büchner's prose fragment _Lenz_ stands apart from everything else in the Büchner house. It is not a play but it is more theatrical than any play: a continuous interior in motion, a body walking through a landscape that mirrors and refuses it. The poet Jakob Michael Reinhold Lenz arrives in the Vosges mountains on the 20th of January and never fully leaves. Staging this work means staging consciousness itself — the thing that holds, the things it holds to, and the moment it can no longer hold.

## Orders

1. **The Playwright (buechner)** shall model the full ENACTS structure for _Lenz_ in German, producing a company of 44 files that give equal dramatic weight to places, pieces, personas, and processes.
2. **The Playwright (buechner)** shall write in the voice Büchner established: spare, physical, without sentimentality, grounded in the specific German of the source. No passages copied; the voice inhabited.
3. **The Theatre Manager (franzos)** shall ensure every file conforms to canon, all links resolve, line endings are correct, and all tests pass before the PR is opened.

## Implementation

- **Location:** All files must reside in the `plays/lenz/` directory.
- **Language:** Play content is in German. Administrative files (`order_260607_02.md`, `REFERENCES.md`) are in English.
- **Density:** Each of the 6 places carries exactly 3 pieces.
- **Source:** Georg Büchner, _Lenz_ (1835, fragment). Public domain.
