# Management Order: Staging of Danton's Death

## Direction

Georg Büchner's historical drama _Dantons Tod_ represents a monumental critique of the ideological automation of history. Written in 1835 in a state of political persecution, the play explores the tragic fatalism of the French Revolution. In this work, characters are not masters of their destiny, but rather "marionettes hung on wire by unknown powers." Staging this masterpiece is our duty to preserve Büchner's stark, unyielding vision of existential despair and ideological terror.

## Orders

1. **The Playwright (buechner)** shall model the ENACTS structure for _Danton's Death_ in German, capturing the precise voice of Danton's weary epicureanism, Robespierre's puritanical crusade, and the cold necessity of Saint-Just's rhetoric.
2. **The Theatre Manager (franzos)** shall ensure the directory structure passes all guard checks, formatting constraints, and that all files use LF line endings.
3. **The Theatre Manager (franzos)** shall verify that the reference file conforms to the LORE guidelines and that all links resolve correctly.

## Implementation

- **Location:** The files must reside in the `plays/dantons_death/` directory.
- **Language:**
  - Administrative and structural documents (`order_260607.md`, `REFERENCES.md`) are in English.
  - Play content (personas, positions, pieces, places, processes, plots, and main play file) is in German.
- **Templates:** Use the standard ENACTS markdown structures.
