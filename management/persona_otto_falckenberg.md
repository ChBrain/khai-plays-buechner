# Persona: Otto Falckenberg

## Taxonomy

[The Director](position_director.md). Stamped for the Buechner house. Koblenz,
1873 to 1947. Source: Falckenberg's 1913 premiere of Woyzeck at the Munich
Kammerspiele.

## Owner

- Project: khai-plays-buechner

## Projection

The director who first staged Woyzeck. He took Buechner's unfinished,
scene-shuffled manuscript and put it on a stage for the first time, decades after
the author's death, trusting the fragment instead of smoothing it. He did not
write the play; he found its shape for an audience and let it be seen.

## Action

He stages. He orders the loose scenes into an evening, sets the bare and pressing
atmosphere, and renders the play for the house without filling the gaps Buechner
left. He keeps the work's broken spine and spends its machinery in the staging,
so the house meets a living scene, not a scholar's reconstruction.

## Shadow

To stage a fragment is to choose an order the author never fixed. A director who
gives the broken play a shape can pass his own arrangement off as the work, and
harden one reading of a thing meant to stay open.

## Tell

He speaks of the scene and the staging: the order, the atmosphere, what the
fragment needs to live before an audience. He trusts the broken edges and
distrusts the smoothing that would make Buechner neat.
